Introduction

Developed in the 1970s, the Standard Model is a particle physics theory describing three out of four fundamental forces - electromagnetic, strong and weak interactions. It classifies all known elementary particles in our universe. Believed to be theoretically self-consistent, it has demonstrated huge successes in providing experimental predictions and has predicted many phenomena with great accuracy. For example, it predicted the existence of undiscovered particles such as the W and Z Bosons, which were experimentally discovered in 1983, where the Standard Model precisely predicted the ratio of their masses.

Unfortunately, to date, the Standard Model still fails to be a complete theory as some phenomena could not be explained. This will be further explained under Limitations!
